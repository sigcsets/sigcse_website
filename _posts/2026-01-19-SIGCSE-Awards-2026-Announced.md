The SIGCSE Board is honored to announce the 2026 ACM SIGCSE Award winners:

- The **2026 SIGCSE Award for Outstanding Contribution to Computer Science Education** is awarded to [Richard E. Ladner](https://www.cs.washington.edu/people/faculty/ladner-richard/), Professor Emeritus, Paul G. Allen School of Computer Science & Engineering, University of Washington, USA.

- The **2026 SIGCSE Award for Distinguished Service to the Computer Science Education Community** is awarded to [Paul Tymann](https://www.cs.rit.edu/~ptt/), Professor Emeritus, Rochester Institute of Technology, USA.

- The **2026 SIGCSE Award for Broadening Participation in Computing Education** is awarded to [Bethany Edmunds](https://www.khoury.northeastern.edu/people/bethany-edmunds/), Associate Dean of Computing Programs and Teaching Professor at Khoury College of Computer Sciences, Northeastern University, USA.

- The **2026 ACM SIGCSE Test of Time Award** is awarded to the paper Online Python Tutor: Embeddable Web-Based Program Visualization for CS Education by Philip J. Guo, In Proceedings of the 44th ACM Technical Symposium on Computer Science Education, Association for Computing Machinery, New York, NY, USA, 579-584. [DOI Link](https://doi.org/10.1145/2445196.2445368)

The Outstanding Contribution, Distinguished Service, and Test of Time awards will be recognized at the [2026 SIGCSE Technical Symposium](https://sigcse2026.sigcse.org/) in St Louis, Missouri, USA, 18 Feb - 21 February 2026. The Broadening Participation award will be recognized at the [2026 31st Annual ACM Conference on Innovation and Technology in Computer Science Education (ITiCSE)](https://iticse.acm.org/2026/) in Madrid, Spain, 13-15 July 2026.

Please join us in congratulating these outstanding members of the SIGCSE community.

Thanks also go out to the members of the Awards committees.

For more information about these awards, including how to nominate someone, and lists of previous winners, please visit [the Awards page on the SIGCSE website](http://sigcse.org/programs/awards/).

Congratulations again to the 2026 ACM SIGCSE Awardees!
